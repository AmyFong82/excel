Children & Adolescents Programs

  • Intensive In-Home (IIH)

    Our Intensive In-Home (IIH) service is designed to support children and adolescents (up to age 20) who struggle with serious and chronic emotional, behavioral, or substance use disorders. These individuals often require intensive interventions to remain stable within their community. This medically necessary service directly addresses their mental health or substance use disorder needs, as evidenced by a diagnosable disturbance documented in the Comprehensive Clinical Assessment and the Person-Centered Plan (PCP).

    Key Features of IIH Services:

    • 24/7 Availability: Our team provides round-theclock support, 365 days a year, ensuring continuous care and immediate response.
    • Time-Limited and Intensive: Services are most intensive at the beginning and gradually decrease as the child's skills and stability improve.
    • Crisis Intervention: Immediate response to diffuse current crises and prevent out-of-home placement.
    • Community Linkage: Ensuring connection to community services and resources for comprehensive support.
    • Family Involvement: Active participation from parents or caregivers is crucial, with services developed in full partnership with the family. Prevent out-of-home placement.

    Service Delivery:

    • Individualized Care: Each child receives personalized services based on their specific needs, delivered in their living environment.
    • Cultural Sensitivity: Our team emphasizes effective engagement with families, respecting and incorporating cultural sensitivities.

    Our Intensive In-Home service is dedicated to providing the essential support and interventions needed to help children and adolescents achieve stability and success within their communities. Contact us to learn more about how we can help your child on their path to recovery and stability.

  • Substance Abuse Intensive Outpatient Program (SAIOP)

    The Substance Abuse Intensive Outpatient Program (SAIOP) is a structured outpatient program designed to help children and adolescents (ages 5-17) begin their recovery journey and develop essential skills for maintaining sobriety. Our program operates a minimum of 3 hours a day, at least 3 days a week, ensuring no more than 2 consecutive days between sessions.

    Our comprehensive SAIOP services include:

    • Individual Counseling and Support: Personalized sessions to address each participant's unique needs.
    • Group Counseling and Support: Collaborative group sessions to foster peer support and shared learning.
    • Family Counseling, Training, or Support: Engaging families in the recovery process to strengthen support systems.
    • Biochemical Assays: Drug screening tests (e.g., urine drug screens) to monitor recent drug use.
    • Relapse Prevention Strategies: Techniques to prevent relapse, incorporating community and social support systems.
    • Life Skills Training: Essential skills for daily living and independence.
    • Crisis Contingency Planning: Preparing for and managing potential crises.
    • Disease Management: Education and strategies for managing addiction as a chronic disease.
    • Adapted Treatment Support Activities: Tailored support for participants with physical disabilities, cooccurring mental health and substance use disorders, or intellectual and developmental disabilities.

    Our program can be customized for specific groups, such as adolescents with co-occurring disorders, those living with HIV, or individuals with similar cognitive functioning levels. Group counseling is provided daily to ensure consistent support and engagement.Join our SAIOP program to help your child or adolescent take crucial steps toward a healthier, substance-free future.

  • Creative Activities

    In addition to traditional therapy, our program offers a wide array of creative activities to foster self-expression and personal growth:

    Music Therapy:

    Participants explore the healing power of music through guided exercises, songwriting, and musical expression.

    Mentoring and Tutoring:

    Participants receive individualized support and guidance from mentors and tutors to excel academically and personally.

    Animation, Recording Studio Time, and Writing Music:

    Participants unleash their creativity through animation, music production, and songwriting in our state-of-the-art studios.

    Art Therapy and Becoming an Author:

    Participants express themselves through various art mediums and even have the opportunity to become published authors.

    Video Editing, Script Writing, Movie Production, and Commercial Creation:

    Participants learn valuable skills in storytelling, media production, and marketing.

    Drama Class, Podcast and Media:

    Participants explore theater, podcasting, and digital media to cultivate confidence and communication skills.

    Athletic Sports:

    Engage in physical activities like basketball, soccer, dance or yoga to promote physical health and well-being while fostering teamwork and discipline.
